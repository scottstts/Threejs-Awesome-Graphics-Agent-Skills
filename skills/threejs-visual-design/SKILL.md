# Three.js Visual Design

Improve the scene in dependency order: composition, form, material roles, lighting, then effects.

## Workflow

1. Define the visual premise in one sentence: subject, mood, material language, camera, and contrast strategy.
2. Identify the hero, supporting forms, background, and negative space.
3. Block the camera and large masses before surface detail.
4. Author recognizable silhouettes and scale cues.
5. Establish foreground, midground, and background separation.
6. Build a reusable shape and prop language rather than repeating arbitrary primitives.
7. Assign a restrained color and material-role palette.
8. Test at gameplay distance, thumbnail size, and motion—not only close-up.
9. Add particles, bloom, fog, and detail only when they reinforce hierarchy.

## Non-negotiable taste rules

- Do not call a primitive stack “premium” because it glows.
- Avoid uniform detail density. Concentrate detail around focal and interactive areas.
- Use repeated motifs with controlled variation in scale, orientation, spacing, and material.
- Preserve readable value groups; color alone is insufficient hierarchy.
- Give surfaces believable thickness, bevel response, joins, contact, and construction logic.
- Use fog and darkness to create depth, not conceal missing content.
- Keep interactive objects legible against the environment.
