# Composition and art direction

## Visual premise

Define:

- focal subject;
- camera relationship;
- dominant shape family;
- material contrast;
- palette and value range;
- depth strategy;
- motion signature.

Example: “A low, wide camera follows a compact ceramic rover through monumental oxidized-metal arches, with warm practical lights against a cool fogged distance.”

## Composition pass

1. Render in grayscale and inspect value hierarchy.
2. Reduce the frame to five large masses.
3. Confirm the focal subject reads at thumbnail size.
4. Check tangent collisions and accidental alignments.
5. Add foreground occlusion and distant scale cues.
6. Preserve breathing room around interactive targets.

## Shape language

Assign meanings:

- round: friendly, soft, safe, organic;
- square: stable, industrial, reliable;
- triangular: directional, fast, hazardous;
- thin vertical: fragile, elegant, tall;
- broad horizontal: grounded, calm, heavy.

Combine primary, secondary, and tertiary forms. Tertiary detail cannot rescue a weak primary silhouette.

## Color hierarchy

Use a restrained base palette, a material-neutral range, and one or two functional accents. Reserve the highest saturation or luminance contrast for focus, state, reward, or danger.

## Readability tests

- active camera and worst-case camera;
- bright and dark displays;
- color-vision deficiency simulation when color carries state;
- motion blur in human perception, even if the renderer has none;
- mobile viewport with HUD and touch controls.
