# Visual scorecard

Score 0–3 with evidence from representative active states.

| Category | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| art direction | none | palette/theme only | coherent form/material/UI language | distinctive identity throughout |
| composition | unclear | basic framing | strong focal/depth hierarchy | memorable and robust across states |
| hero subject | placeholder | styled primitive | authored silhouette and states | expressive, layered, iconic |
| environment | empty | repeated filler | layered prop/world system | dense, authored, readable world |
| materials | flat/default | basic PBR | coherent roles and variation | rich, controlled, scale-correct |
| lighting | default/unreadable | mood but weak form | intentional shape/readability | cinematic and consistently legible |
| motion/VFX | absent/random | generic | event-driven and clear | expressive, cohesive, performant |
| UI/interaction | missing/debug | functional | responsive hierarchy and states | polished, integrated, accessible |
| performance evidence | none | subjective | metrics and target-device checks | before/after budgets and stress tests |

## Automatic blockers

- primitive-dominant hero or world;
- bloom, darkness, or fog hiding missing design;
- active interaction cannot be understood;
- UI overlaps critical content or touch areas;
- no active-state evidence;
- no performance evidence after major visual changes;
- critical console, loading, resize, or restart failures.

## Report

List score, evidence, and highest-leverage improvement for every category. Do not average away a critical blocker.
