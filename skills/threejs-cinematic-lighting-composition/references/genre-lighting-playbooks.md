# Genre lighting playbooks

Use these as starting hypotheses, not presets.

## Product/configurator

- neutral or branded environment;
- broad highlights revealing curvature;
- clean contact and grounding;
- controlled reflection cards;
- stable exposure across variants;
- minimal atmospheric effects;
- camera paths that preserve inspection.

## Stylized adventure

- strong value grouping;
- directional sun/key;
- simplified material response;
- color-scripted regions;
- landmark lighting;
- readable character rim;
- atmospheric separation without muddy shadows.

## Horror

- restricted visibility with explicit player information;
- motivated practicals;
- negative space and occlusion;
- selective contrast rather than global darkness;
- restrained camera;
- threat telegraph through sound, silhouette, motion, and local value.

## Science fiction

- distinguish functional emissives from decoration;
- use material and shape language before neon;
- controlled cool/warm machinery contrast;
- volumetric shafts only where source and occluders support them;
- specular structure from environment panels;
- HUD/UI hierarchy independent of world clutter.

## Racing/action

- expose the next decision;
- strong road/path contrast;
- distant landmarks;
- speed-readable parallax;
- controlled motion blur;
- sun/glare that does not hide hazards;
- effects biased toward edges and wake, not aim path.

## Diorama/isometric

- readable top planes and silhouettes;
- shadow direction that describes height;
- restrained perspective or orthographic framing;
- material grouping at small scale;
- controlled ambient occlusion/contact;
- background with lower contrast and frequency.

## Abstract/generative

- one governing visual rule;
- hierarchy through scale, rhythm, color, and motion;
- stable anti-aliasing;
- avoid equal-frequency noise;
- use post-processing as part of the rule, not decoration.
